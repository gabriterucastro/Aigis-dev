Gravitational Slope (simulated 4.00-hour rotation)

Hypothetical slope if Ryugu rotated twice as fast as today

Slope computed assuming Ryugu rotates once every 4.00 hours — faster than today's 7.63 hours. Stronger centrifugal force at the equator reduces the effective gravity there, lowering the slope angle at the equatorial ridge compared to the current 7.63-hour case.

Comparing with the current slope shows how YORP spin-down (or spin-up in the past) changes the surface stress pattern and may explain the equatorial ridge formation.
