Geopotential Height

Effective topographic height accounting for gravity and rotation — where material naturally flows

Geopotential height = effective gravitational potential ÷ surface acceleration. Unlike physical elevation, it represents the "thermodynamic height" — how much energy a particle needs to move between two surface points. Material spontaneously flows from high to low geopotential height, just like water flows downhill on Earth.

On Ryugu, the equatorial region has lower geopotential height because rotation reduces the effective potential there — this drove material migration toward the equatorial ridge.
