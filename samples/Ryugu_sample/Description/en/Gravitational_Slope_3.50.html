Gravitational Slope (simulated 3.50-hour rotation)

Near-critical spin simulation — mass-shedding threshold

Critical spin condition: At 3.50-hour rotation, centrifugal force at Ryugu's equator nearly cancels gravitational attraction. Equatorial slope approaches 0° — material on the equatorial surface is barely held by gravity and would be shed into space with any faster spin.

This simulation represents the spin-up limit for a rubble-pile body with Ryugu's density (~1.19 g/cm³). The critical period for a rubble pile is roughly 3–4 hours depending on density and cohesion.

If Ryugu was once spun up by YORP to near this rate, the equatorial ridge would have formed as material migrated from high latitudes toward the equator and settled there as the spin rate changed. This map provides the "end member" scenario for that process.
