Gravitational Slope

Current rotation period (7.63 hours)

Gravitational slope is the angle between the surface normal and the direction of the effective gravity vector (gravitational attraction + centrifugal force from rotation). It indicates how "steep" the surface is from a particle's perspective — the direction a ball would roll.

High slope (red) → steep surface, material cannot accumulate.
Low slope (blue) → flat surface, regolith settles and accumulates.
