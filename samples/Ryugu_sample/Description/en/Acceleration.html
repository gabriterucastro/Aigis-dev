Surface Acceleration

Magnitude of effective gravitational acceleration at each surface point

Acceleration here is the magnitude of the effective surface gravity — the vector sum of gravitational attraction and centrifugal acceleration from Ryugu's rotation. It varies across the surface because Ryugu is not a perfect sphere and rotation creates a latitude-dependent centrifugal component.

Ryugu's surface gravity is roughly 0.11–0.15 mm/s² — about 70,000× weaker than Earth's 9.8 m/s².
