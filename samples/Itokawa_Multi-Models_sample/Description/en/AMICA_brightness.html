AMICA Brightness

Surface reflectance map from Hayabusa's AMICA multiband imager

AMICA (Asteroid Multiband Imaging Camera) on Hayabusa captured images in 8 bands (0.38–1.01 μm). This map shows the reflectance (brightness) of Itokawa's surface in the v-band (visible light). Bright areas reflect more sunlight; dark areas absorb more.

Brightness variations reveal mineralogy, space weathering degree, and surface texture differences across the surface.

Space weathering: Hayabusa returned particles confirmed nanoscale metallic iron inclusions at the outermost surface (few nm depth), formed by solar wind implantation. These particles cause spectral reddening and darkening — the mechanism of space weathering revealed for the first time.

v-band reflectance (visual), normalized per Hapke photometric model. AMICA instrument: 8-band filter wheel, field of view 5.83° × 5.69°.
